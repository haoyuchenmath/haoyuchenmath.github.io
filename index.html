I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mathematics, the University of Utah. Previously, I obtained my M.S. and B.A. in Statistics from University of Minnesota in 2021 and from UC Berkeley in 2019. I am a U.S. citizen.

My research focuses on geometry and manifold learning, with an emphasis on developing interpretable geometric models and distance metrics grounded in rigorous theory and supported by efficient algorithms. I leverage geometric perspectives to provide novel insights, enhance model performance, and design methods that are both mathematically principled and computationally practical.
